Dervis Sušić

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Derviš Sušić (3 June 1925 – 1 September 1990) was a Bosnian writer, known best for his first work I, Danilo.[1] His political affiliations and life path had a significant impact on the themes of his literary works.

Sušić was a Yugoslav Partisan during World War II and a communist in Yugoslavia.[2][3]
